## D. 12-month P&L projection (conservative ramp)

| Quarter | Sale deals | Rental placements | Mgmt units (cumul.) | Revenue | Op. costs* | Net (pre-owner-draw) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Q1 (setup) | 0 | 1 | 1 | ~600 | ~4,200 | **−3,600** |
| Q2 | 2 | 4 | 4 | ~8,400 | ~4,500 | **+3,900** |
| Q3 | 4 | 6 | 8 | ~16,400 | ~6,000 | **+10,400** |
| Q4 | 6 | 8 | 12 | ~23,800 | ~7,500 | **+16,300** |
| **Year 1 total** | **12** | **19** | **12 active** | **≈ 49,200** | **≈ 22,200** | **≈ +27,000** |

\* Op. costs exclude one-time setup (Section A) and owner draw. Q1 is light; costs rise as
you add marketing spend and (late Q3/Q4) a first hire.

**Read of the numbers:**
- Year 1 is about **proving the niche and building a referral + management book**, not
  maximizing profit. Net ~€27k *before* your own salary is a realistic conservative base.
- Upside levers: dual-side representation, commercial/higher-value deals, the recurring
  **rental-management** book (compounds into Year 2), and diaspora "relocation package" fees.
- Break-even on monthly burn lands around **Q2** in this conservative model.

## E. Sensitivity (what moves the result)
- **+50% deal count** (18 sales) → revenue ≈ €63k, net ≈ €38k.
- **Add 30 mgmt units by year-end** → +~€7k–10k recurring, mostly Year-2 visible.
- **Higher avg ticket (foreign buyers skew premium/central)** → each +€50k on price adds
  ~€1,000/deal at 2%. Targeting the foreign segment likely lifts avg fee above the €2,900 base.
