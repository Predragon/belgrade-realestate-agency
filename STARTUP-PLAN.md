# Belgrade Real Estate Agency — Startup Plan

*Working draft · Prepared June 2026 · All figures in EUR unless noted*

> **Concept in one line:** An English-first, foreigner-focused real estate brokerage in
> central Belgrade, run by a returning Serbian-American with 20 years of U.S. sales,
> rental, and investment experience — serving the segment local agencies serve worst.

---

## 1. The opportunity

Belgrade's prime, ready-to-occupy stock is the most liquid segment of the market, and
foreign demand has an outsized effect on it. Yet most Belgrade agencies serve foreign
buyers poorly in English and can't explain the *reciprocity rule*, company-purchase
workaround, or residency angle in a way a relocating American or EU buyer trusts.

**That gap is the business.** Native English + native Serbian + real U.S. brokerage
experience is a combination almost no competing agency offers.

### Target segments (in priority order)
1. **Foreign individual buyers** — relocators, remote workers, diaspora returning home,
   lifestyle/second-home buyers. Highest-margin, least-served.
2. **Foreign investors** — buy-and-hold for the 10-year capital-gains exemption; small
   portfolios; off-plan new-builds. Plays directly to your U.S. investment background.
3. **Rental management for foreign owners** — recurring revenue; owners abroad need a
   trusted local manager. Sticky and compounding.
4. **Local sellers with foreign-friendly inventory** — to build listing supply you can
   market to segments 1–3.

## 3. Legal & licensing path

**Entity:** Register a **d.o.o.** (LLC equivalent) with the APR (Serbian Business
Registers Agency). A d.o.o. also lets you offer the *company-purchase workaround* to
clients who lack reciprocity, and limits personal liability. (A *preduzetnik* /
sole-trader is cheaper but riskier and weaker for B2B credibility — confirm with
accountant.)

**Brokerage license — to enter the Register of Real Estate Agents (Ministry of Trade,
decision within 30 days), you need:**
1. **Business premises** — a registered office (required, not optional).
2. **Professional liability insurance** from a Serbia-based insurer. *Confirm current
   minimum coverage with a lawyer — it changes.*
3. **Professional competence** — at least one founder/employee who has passed the
   **broker exam** (Serbian Chamber of Commerce, 60% pass mark; topics: property rights,
   contracts, brokerage law). License valid 5 years, then renew.

**Action item:** book a 1-hour consult with a Serbian real estate attorney (Zunic Law or
Stojković Attorneys — both work in English) to confirm insurance minimums, exam/experience
prerequisites for a returning citizen, and to stand up the d.o.o.

## 4. Tax frame (affects how we advise clients and structure our own deals)
- Transfer tax (resale): **2.5%**; new-builds carry VAT instead.
- Annual property tax: **~0.4%**.
- Rental income: **20%** (deductions for maintenance, interest).
- Capital gains: **15%**, **zero after 10-year hold** — our core investor story.
- **No U.S.–Serbia income-tax treaty** — pair with a cross-border tax advisor for U.S. clients.

## 5. Phased roadmap

**Phase 0 — Setup (Months 1–3)**
- Attorney consult; register d.o.o.; secure office; bind insurance; pass/schedule broker
  exam; register in Agent Register.
- Build English-first brand: name, logo, website, CRM, listing-portal accounts
  (nekretnine.rs, 4zida, halooglasi, City Expert).
- Line up partner network: attorney, accountant, mortgage contact, notary, contractor/stager.

**Phase 1 — First listings & first deals (Months 4–6)**
- Win 8–15 listings (focus on foreign-friendly central inventory).
- Launch content engine: English guides ("How a foreigner buys in Belgrade", "The
  reciprocity rule explained", "Investing for the 10-year exemption").
- Target first 2–4 closed transactions.

**Phase 2 — Recurring revenue (Months 7–12)**
- Stand up **rental management** arm for foreign owners (recurring fee base).
- Hire first agent/admin once deal flow justifies it.
- SEO + referral flywheel from satisfied foreign buyers.

**Phase 3 — Scale (Year 2+)**
- 2–3 agents; expand to Novi Sad/Niš inventory for investors (better yields).
- Productized "relocation + purchase + residency" package for diaspora and remote workers.

## 6. Revenue model (illustrative)

- Avg Belgrade transaction ~**€135k–156k**; at **2% per side**, our side ≈ **€2,700–3,100**
  per deal (more when we represent both sides or on higher-value/commercial deals).
- Rental placement ≈ **one month's rent** (~€400–900).
- Rental management ≈ **8–12% of monthly rent**, recurring.

**Conservative Year 1 target:** 12–18 sale-side transactions + 15–25 rental placements +
a small management book → see `BUDGET.md` for the P&L.

## 7. Key risks & mitigations
| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Title/cadastre irregularities (common on older bldgs) | Always run attorney due diligence on *katastar* before listing/closing. |
| Insurance minimum / licensing rules shift | Confirm with attorney before committing; budget a buffer. |
| Slow ramp / cash burn | Keep fixed costs lean (small office, outsourced accounting); hold 6-month runway. |
| Foreign-buyer demand is segment-specific | Diversify into rental mgmt (recurring) + local listings. |
| No U.S.–Serbia tax treaty surprises clients | Bundle a vetted cross-border tax advisor into the offering. |
| Solo-founder bandwidth | Productize/templatize early; hire admin once deal flow proven. |
