# U.S. vs. Serbia Real Estate Taxes: What American Buyers Need to Know (2026)

If you've invested in U.S. real estate, you know the tax code is half the game — depreciation,
1031 exchanges, the mortgage interest deduction, the primary-residence exclusion. So the
natural question when you look at Belgrade is: **how does the tax math actually compare?**

This guide answers that head-to-head. We've spent 20 years working in U.S. real estate and

> **The short version:** The U.S. system rewards leverage, deductions, and deferral — and is

> benefit: **zero capital gains tax after a 10-year hold.** Different philosophies, and the

## Two different philosophies

### The U.S.: rewards leverage, deductions, and deferral
The power of the U.S. system isn't low rates — it's the *machinery*. You depreciate the
building against the rent you actually collect, deduct your mortgage interest, defer gains
indefinitely through 1031 exchanges, and your heirs receive a stepped-up basis that erases
the gain entirely at death. A sophisticated investor can legally pay very little current tax
on a growing portfolio. The price of admission is **complexity** and, in many states, high
transaction and holding costs.

### Serbia: rewards simplicity and patience
Serbia's rates are low and flat, transaction costs are modest, and the standout feature is
beautifully simple: **hold longer than 10 years and pay zero capital gains tax.** There's no
depreciation game, no 1031, no mortgage shelter — but you also don't need a team of CPAs to
benefit. It's a buy-and-hold investor's regime.

## Where each one wins

**Serbia is better for:**
- **Long-term buy-and-hold** — the 10-year 0% beats U.S. 15–20%+ (plus NIIT, plus state) at sale.
- **Low ongoing carry** — ~0.4% annual property tax vs. a ~1.1% U.S. average.
- **Simplicity** — flat rates, low compliance, cheap entry (2.5%).
- **Clean rental income** — flat 20% with deductions, no recapture later.

**The U.S. is better for:**
- **Active, leveraged investors** — depreciation + interest can shelter rental income to near zero on paper.
- **Serial traders** — 1031 compounds gains tax-deferred across many deals.
- **Owner-occupiers selling a home** — the $250k/$500k exclusion is very generous.
- **Estate planning** — stepped-up basis at death is a uniquely powerful U.S. edge.

## The cross-border catch every American must know

**There is no U.S.–Serbia income tax treaty.** This is the single most important practical
point for any American buying in Serbia:

- The U.S. taxes citizens and residents on **worldwide income**, so Serbian rental income and
  gains are **also reportable on your U.S. return.**
- Without a treaty, you rely on the **foreign tax credit** — not treaty provisions — to avoid
  double taxation, and the mechanics don't always line up cleanly.
- The clearest example: Serbia's **0% gain after 10 years** means there's **no Serbian tax to
  credit**, so the U.S. may still tax that gain. The Serbian benefit doesn't automatically
  flow through to your U.S. bill.

**Bottom line:** the Serbian side can be wonderfully simple, but the U.S. side never sleeps.
Work with a cross-border tax advisor from the start — we make that introduction as part of

## A worked illustration (buy-and-hold)
You buy a central Belgrade apartment for **€150,000**, hold it **11 years**, and sell for

- **Serbian tax on the gain:** **€0** (held over 10 years).
- **A comparable U.S. sale:** 15–20% federal capital gains + 3.8% NIIT (if applicable) + state

- **U.S. citizen, the catch:** because Serbia charges nothing, there's no foreign credit, so
  the gain may still face U.S. tax — exactly why cross-border planning matters.
