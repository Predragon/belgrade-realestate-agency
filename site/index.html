Good to know

Questions foreign buyers always ask

Can foreigners buy property in Belgrade?

Most can — Serbia uses a reciprocity rule, and we confirm your status up front. If your country doesn't qualify, a Serbian company can buy on your behalf, completely legally. Agricultural land is the one exception reserved for citizens.

Do I need to be in Belgrade to buy?

No. You can buy by power of attorney, and many of our clients close the entire purchase from abroad.

What does it cost beyond the price?

Typically a 2.5% transfer tax on resale (VAT on new-builds), roughly 0.4% annual property tax, and our commission at standard market rate. We lay out every cost before you commit.

Will buying give me residency?

Not automatically, but property ownership supports a temporary-residence application — and we can help you with that process.

I'm American — what about taxes?

There's no U.S.–Serbia income-tax treaty, so we introduce you to a cross-border tax advisor to keep both sides clean and avoid double taxation.
