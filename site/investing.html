Investor's guide

Investing in Belgrade & the 10-Year Exemption

The quietest, most powerful number in Serbian real estate — and how to build a portfolio around it.

Most foreign investors come for the headline numbers — solid yields, a maturing market, prices below Western Europe. Those are real. But the most powerful rule is one most people overlook.

Hold a property in Serbia for more than 10 years, and you pay zero capital gains tax when you sell.

The tax picture for investors (2026)

TaxRateWhat it means for you
Capital gains15%Drops to 0% after a 10-year hold — the core of the strategy
Rental income20%Deductions allowed for maintenance, mortgage interest
Transfer tax (resale)2.5%One-time, at purchase (new-builds carry VAT)
Annual property tax~0.4%Recurring holding cost

Why the 10-year rule matters so much

Buy a central Belgrade apartment for €150,000, hold a decade of steady ~5–6% growth, and it's worth roughly €245,000 — about €95,000 of gain.

The same gain, taxed two different ways

15% on a €95k gain ≈ €14,250 owed at year 9 — versus zero at year 10. Figures illustrative; appreciation not guaranteed.

The income side: rental yields while you hold

The exemption rewards patience — and rental income pays you to be patient. Belgrade's prime, central, move-in-ready apartments are the most liquid rental stock, supported by remote workers, business demand, and diaspora tenants. Rental income is taxed at 20%, but you deduct real expenses. Secondary cities like Novi Sad and Niš can offer stronger yields.

The combined play: collect taxed-but-deductible rental income for 10+ years, then exit with zero capital gains tax. Income while you wait, tax-free appreciation when you sell.

The market backdrop (2026)

€2,600/m² Belgrade average 5–6% annual growth (maturing, not a bubble) 7–9% projected for top new-builds

A maturing, steadily-appreciating market is exactly the environment a 10-year strategy wants — you're not betting on a moonshot, you're compounding a stable asset while the tax code works for you.

How investors structure the purchase

  1. Buy as an individual. Simplest; works well for a single property and clean reciprocity. The 10-year exemption applies to individuals.
  2. Buy through a Serbian company (d.o.o.). Preferred for multiple properties, lack of reciprocity, or combining property with business/residency goals — with its own tax treatment and accounting, so decide deliberately.

U.S. investors: there's no U.S.–Serbia income-tax treaty, so coordinate Serbian and U.S. obligations with a cross-border tax advisor. See our U.S. vs. Serbia tax comparison. We make the introduction.

A framework for evaluating a Belgrade investment

  1. Location & liquidity — easy to rent and easy to resell.
  2. Clean title & legalization — verified before you commit.
  3. Yield — realistic net income after the 20% tax and expenses.
  4. Hold horizon — is 10+ years realistic? If yes, the exemption is yours.
  5. Exit demand — who buys this in 10 years? Prime central stock has the deepest pool.

General information, not legal, tax, or investment advice. Returns and appreciation are not guaranteed, and tax rules (including the exemption) can change. We confirm current specifics for your situation before you commit.
