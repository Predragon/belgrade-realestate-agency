Buyer's guide

Belgrade Neighborhood Guide for Foreign Buyers

Where you buy changes both your lifestyle and your liquidity at exit. An honest, plain-English tour.

"Where in Belgrade should I buy?" is the question right after "Can I buy?" — and the one where local knowledge earns its keep. The right answer depends on whether you're buying to live, to rent out, or to hold and appreciate.

Citywide context (2026): Belgrade averages ~€2,600/m², with central, river-facing, move-in-ready stock commanding the strongest premiums — and the deepest foreign-buyer and tenant demand.

The central core — highest demand, strongest liquidity

Stari Grad (Old Town)

Historic heart — Knez Mihailova, Kalemegdan, elegant pre-war buildings, walkable everything.

Lifestyle · premium rentals

Vračar

Belgrade's beloved residential favorite — leafy streets, cafés, the Temple of Saint Sava, strong neighborhood feel.

Owner-occupiers · long-term rental

Dorćol

Cool, central, slightly bohemian, sloping to the Danube — bars, restaurants, young professionals.

Lifestyle · strong rental appeal

Savski Venac (Senjak, Dedinje)

Green, embassy-and-villa territory — among the most prestigious addresses in the city.

Premium · luxury

The riverfront & modern Belgrade

Belgrade Waterfront

Flagship riverside development — modern towers, promenade, the Kula landmark. Polished and internationally marketed.

Turnkey modern · easy resale

Novi Beograd (New Belgrade)

Organized west-bank district — business towers, wide boulevards, shopping, modern and older blocks.

Investors · professional tenants

Zemun

Historic riverside town-within-the-city on the Danube — Gardoš tower, quay restaurants, relaxed pace.

Character · value

Zvezdara · Voždovac · Banovo Brdo

Solid residential value — green, family-friendly, better price per m² with dependable everyday-tenant demand.

Value · yield · families

How to choose — a simple decision frame

  • Buying to LIVE: prioritize lifestyle fit — Vračar, Dorćol, Stari Grad for walkable city life; Senjak/Dedinje for quiet prestige; Banovo Brdo/Voždovac for green value.
  • Buying to RENT OUT: prioritize liquidity and tenant pool — central, New Belgrade (professionals), and the Waterfront (premium/short-term).
  • Buying to HOLD & APPRECIATE: prioritize resale demand at exit — prime, central, foreign-friendly stock has the broadest future buyer pool.

Three rules that apply everywhere

  1. Title and legalization trump the postcode. A great area doesn't fix a building with permit problems — we verify the cadastre first.
  2. The building and floor matter as much as the area. Renovation, elevator, heating, orientation swing value within the same street.
  3. Buy where you can also sell and rent. Liquidity is a feature.
