The Reciprocity Rule Explained

Can you actually buy property in Serbia? The answer hinges on one concept — and it's simpler than it sounds.

Of all the questions foreign buyers ask, this is the first and most important: "Am I even allowed to buy?" The answer in Serbia hinges on a single concept — reciprocity.

Reciprocity in one sentence: If Serbian citizens are allowed to buy property in your country, then you, as a citizen of that country, are allowed to buy property in Serbia.

How reciprocity works — a two-way mirror

Most Western buyers (U.S., EU, UK, Canada, Australia) qualify.

The two ways reciprocity is established

  1. A bilateral agreement (treaty) between Serbia and your country regulating property ownership.
  2. Factual (de facto) reciprocity — no treaty, but in practice Serbian citizens can buy in your country, so the right is recognized in fact.

What if your country doesn't have reciprocity?

This surprises people: a lack of reciprocity is not a dead end. A foreigner can form a Serbian company — typically a d.o.o. (the rough equivalent of an LLC) — and have the company buy the property.

Why it works: the company is a Serbian legal entity. Reciprocity is a rule about foreign individuals; a Serbian company is domestic, so the rule simply doesn't apply. You own the company; the company owns the property.

It's common, works regardless of nationality, can be cleaner for investors holding multiple properties, and supports business and residency pathways. It also has trade-offs — accounting and corporate tax — so it's worth a short conversation.

Why you shouldn't just guess your status

Online lists are often outdated and ignore factual reciprocity. Getting it wrong can mean weeks viewing, negotiating, even depositing on a property you ultimately can't register. We confirm your status with an attorney before we show you a single property.

Quick FAQ

Does buying give me residency?

No — not automatically. But ownership supports a temporary-residence application.

I'm of Serbian descent / a returning citizen — does this restrict me?

If you're a Serbian citizen, reciprocity doesn't restrict you at all — you buy like any local. Dual citizens included.

Is the company route legitimate?

Yes. Forming a d.o.o. to hold property is a standard, lawful structure used by domestic and foreign investors alike.
